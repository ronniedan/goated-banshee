# Goated Banshee

The banshee, a figure in Irish mythology, warns families of death with her distinctive wail. It was also believed that if she threw her comb at you, your death would be imminent. Goated Banshee holds the Bachal Isu, known as the staff of Saint Patrick, that was a symbol of power so great in Ireland that it had to be destroyed. Many Irish mythological creatures have hooved goats feet, notably the Púca, a mischievous shapeshifter (thought to have inspired Shakespeare's Puck in Midsummer Night's Dream). There is also Glaistig, often described as a hybrid fairy or water spirit that is half-woman, half-goat. Creatures like the Púca can take on the form of a fairy (although Irish Fairies are not the small winged creatures of popular folklore).
